About me

Bio Patrícia is a postdoctoral researcher at the Yale School of the Environment, specializing in fire–climate dynamics in tropical ecosystems. She holds a degree in Energy and Environmental Engineering and a joint PhD in Geophysics and Meteorology from the University of Lisbon and the Federal University of Rio de Janeiro. Her doctoral research was supported by a PhD fellowship and received the Portuguese Association of Meteorology and Geophysics award for “Best Doctoral Thesis in Meteorology,” as well as the University of Lisbon’s “Outstanding Doctoral Thesis in Earth Sciences.” She has worked in this field for over a decade, publishing six papers as lead author, five as a co-author, and two book chapters as lead author.
Her work sits at the intersection of Earth observation, climate science, ecology, and policy. She co-authored the United Nations Environment Programme report "Spreading like Wildfire: The Rising Threat of Extraordinary Landscape Fires", and served as a scientific representative at the 2022 UN Ocean Conference and the 2026 UN Forum on Forests. Patrícia is also part of the Science Team for the State of Wildfires Project, which produces an annual report on global extreme fire activity, and has consulted for non-profit organizations on fire risk and modelling, fire management, and carbon emissions in South America.
