Training Schools and Workshops

Forest resilience in climate risk assessments of tropical forests: current knowledge and future research
2025 Yale University

This workshop aimed to better understand forest vulnerability, resistance and resilience to environmental changes which will allow us to estimate potential changes in carbon fluxes, and carbon stocks of tropical forests in the face of climate change. We will focus on three main questions: 1 What are the risk factors affecting tropical forest carbon stocks and ecosystem services in the face of future environmental changes and compound disturbances? 2 What are the drivers of tropical forest resilience following environmental changes and compound disturbances, and how can resilience evaluation and monitoring be integrated into risk assessment frameworks? and 3 What are the key governance, social and economic challenges in monitoring and measuring tropical forest resilience, and how can these be addressed to support forest protection, and feedback mechanisms that influence tropical forest resistance and resilience to climate change.

DAMOCLES Training School on Compound Events
2022 Eötvös Loránd Tudományegyetem

The goal of this training school organized by the COST Action DAMOCLES is to train the next generation of compound event researchers in the modelling of compound events. Compound weather and climate events refer to the combination of multiple climate drivers that contributes to societal or environmental risk. They can pose serious threats to natural systems and human societies. Modelling of compound events requires knowledge on many different drivers and approaches. The school will be a mixture of lectures and project work. The lectures will cover a range of topics including: Introduction to Modelling; Regional Climate Modelling; Event Attribution and several lectures on Impact Modelling. Each participant will spend time working on a group project applying what has been learnt.

Pyrolife TE2 - Understanding fire human dimensions and planning
2021 Fundación de Ecología del Fuego y Gestión de Incendios Pau Costa

Explore the social dynamics shaping wildfires and its consequences in contemporary societies, and how social sciences are relevant to successfully achieve Integrated Fire Management. Participants will gain insight into the social perspective of fires to help them build stronger interdisciplinary PhD projects.

CORDEX Central America and South America Online Paper-Writing Workshop on Regional Climate Modeling
2020 World Climate Research Programme (WCRP)

Promote collaborative activities and networking in the SAM/CAM regions with focus on specific regional climate phenomena. In this context, a virtual workshop on paper-writing is proposed with the aim of enhancing the capacity of PhD students and early career researchers in designing a research topic on regional climate modeling and documenting/writing the scientific results.

C-CASCADES TW4 - Diagenic models and earth system models of intermediate complexity
2017 Université Libre de Bruxelles

This TW provides an introduction to and practical hands-on learning in Earth System Modelling (ESM). It will provide a chance to explore the dynamics of the Earth's climate system, global carbon cycling and the biogeochemical impacts of CO2 emissions in the context of past (and future) climate events.

C-CASCADES TW2 - Regional to global modelling
2017
Centre National de la Recherche Scientifique - Institut Pierre Simon Laplace (CNRS-IPSL)

The main objective of this C-CASCADES Training Workshop is to achieve a better understanding of the biogeochemical modules (land and ocean) from an Earth System Model (ESM). In order to achieve this, the workshop included expert lectures and practical sessions about the ocean model NEMO-PISCES and the land-surface model ORCHIDEE.
